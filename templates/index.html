📚 Educational Information

  • Global Alignment (Needleman-Wunsch): Aligns entire sequences from start to end. Best for sequences of similar length.
  • Local Alignment (Smith-Waterman): Finds the best matching subsequence. Ideal for finding conserved domains.
  • Dynamic Programming: Both algorithms use DP to find optimal alignments by building a scoring matrix.
  • Time Complexity: O(n×m) where n and m are sequence lengths. Space complexity is also O(n×m).
  • Hirschberg Algorithm: An advanced variant that reduces space complexity to O(min(n,m)) using divide-and-conquer.
